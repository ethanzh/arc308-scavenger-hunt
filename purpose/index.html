Purpose

CCTV is the leading state broadcaster of news media in China. It broadcasts all kinds of different media
such as documentaries, comedies, and news. Although their first broadcast was in 1958, CCTV was not officially
launched until 1978. Prior to the construction of the CCTV tower, all of CCTV's operations took place in the
old China Media Group building, pictured below.

In 2001, Beijing (and China as a whole) was growing faster than ever before. This meant the state-run media
was also growing. By 2001, CCTV decided they had outgrown their old building and were in need of a new one
(details about that process can be found here). When the building opened in 2016, it was the
face of a new, more modern China.

Unsurprisingly, this building's main purpose is to house China Central Television. However, the beauty is in the
details, and that is exactly what architect Rem Koolhaas took into account.

The building's two separate towers, the the connector between them all serve different purposes. Tower 1 is where the lobby, editing
area, and offices are. Tower 1 also descends three floors underground, connecting directly to the Beijing railway
system. Tower 2 on the other hand is where the actual broadcasting of news takes place. The connector between the two towers,
dubbed 'The Overhang' is where all administrative work is done.

One might expect the CCTV tower to exclusively be used by office workers employed by CCTV. However it's purpose goes
far beyond that. Yes, of course the main users of the building are the 10,000 CCTV employees (editors, newscasters, journalists to name a few)
but the building is also used by thousands of commuters who use the tower's subway stop to get to work. The architects also
went to great lengths to facilitate public access, wherein visitors can be shown the entirety of the tower, including the history
of CCTV, sights of everyday studio work, as well the amazing views of the rest of Beijing. Because of this, one could say
every resident or visitor to Beijing is a potential user of the building. The outdoor areas of the building are also used for filming
as well as acting as common spaces where employees can relax and interact.

As one of the first modern buildings in Beijing's central business district, one could make the argument that the
CCTV tower affected the lives of everyone living in the area, and also drew people in. One personal anecdote I can add, is
that this area is filled with vendors selling delicious street food (my favorite is a Chinese crepe called 煎饼, pictured below).
I would count merchants such as these as "users" of the building in the sense that they profit and make a living off of the
thousands of employees who go in and out of the building everyday.
