Print Sources

Source 1

Wood, Antony, et al. Best Tall Buildings: a Global Overview of 2013 Skyscrapers: CTBUH Awards.
  Routledge, 2014.

Summary:

The chapter in this book "the power and the glory - strength and elegance in structure" specifically focuses
on the CCTV tower. In particular, it talks about how its form - being one continuous tube system allows for the building
to withstand the substantial seismic and wind events that it experiences. The source also talks about how the building is
reinforced with steel beams, arranged in a particular manner. Although undoubtedly providing a structural service, these
beams also act as a pleasing visual to the rhythm of the building. I also found it interesting that although the 243m tall building
is within the required height limit, it did not meet standards structurally, and had to be looked at in-depth by a panel of
Chinese engineers.

Source 2

Ching, Francis D. K., et al. A Global History of Architecture, John Wiley & Sons, Incorporated, 2010.
  ProQuest Ebook Central, https://ebookcentral-proquest-com.ezproxy.lib.utexas.edu/lib/utxa/detail.action?docID=698776.

Summary:

This source has examines thousands of years of the history of architecture. There is a chapter near the end
focusing on globalization, and China specifically. There are interesting facts here such as the global price
steel doubling due to demand for Chinese skyscrapers. There is also discussion of Rem Koolhaas' book, S, M, L, XL
and his musing about the contemporary city with specifics regard to the importance of scale in architecture. The article then
details some of Koolhaas' most famous works around the world, of course including the CCTV tower in Beijing. Something I found
particularly enlightening was Koolhaas's view that "the usual differences between architecture, the street, and the city are things
of the past. Instead, architecture has to embrace and combine all of these.

Source 3

Ericson, Staffan, and Kristina Riegert. Media Houses: Architecture, Media and the Production of Centrality.
   Peter Lang, 2010.

Summary:

This source discusses several aspects of the CCTV tower and what it represents for China and its future. There is a
big emphasis on the transparency it provides, both metaphorically and physically. The building is designed so that anyone can come
and witness the state-run television being produced, without any secrets. The sources also discusses how this new tower is a
"rebranding" of sorts for CCTV. The building is also designed in such a way that office workers will be more in-tune with what their
colleagues are working on, thereby establishing a greater sense of community within the organization through architecture. There is an
emphasis on the flow of communication between employees that is representative of the flow of information from CCTV itself to the Chinese citizens.
