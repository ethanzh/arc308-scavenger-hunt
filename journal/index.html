Three important things I learned

  1. It has been the decision of the ruling party in China to place an emphasis on commissioning grand architectural projects from
    prestigious foreign architects, such as Rem Koolhaas in this case. Koolhaas is not alone as a foreign architect working in a country
    with little history of democracy. Similar architects such as Norman Foster have also been commissioned to build grand structures in China
    (in Foster's case, Beijing Capital International Airport).
  2. I learned about the cultural significance of the building to China as a whole. There are fears from some that the tower will resemble a
    Chinese version of Hitler's Reich Chancellery. "If there are protests in the Chinese countryside or more upheavals in Tibet, decisions
    about how to cover those events on Chinese television will be made in the Koolhaas building."
  3. Koolhaas has receieved some criticism for taking on the CCTV project, out of fears that he is enabling an autocratic government
    that spies on its people. Koolhaas has refuted this, and says he hopes one day the state-controlled media company will function
    "like the BBC".
