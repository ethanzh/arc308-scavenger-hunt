Firsthand

During the entirety of my living in Beijing, I never actually had to opportunity to go inside and visit the CCTV
tower, however I did have the chance to get very close to it during drives into and out of the city (I lived in the suburbs).

Although it may go without saying, it stands repeating that this building is truly an anomaly amongst the landscape it
inhibits. Although the skyline is changing these days with buildings such as China Zun (pictured below) being built literally right across the
street from the CCTV tower, the majority of the surrounding buildings are what we might think of as 'typical'.

What I really like about the area that the CCTV is located in (the central business district) is that it's a clash of extremes.
While you are surrounded by glass-covered skyscrapers in one spot, you can simply walk two blocks down the road and be right back on a
typical shopping street, with tons of options for dining, shopping, etc.

What could previously have been described as "just another business district in a big city" has been fundamentally changed
by the inclusion of the CCTV tower. It now gives the entire area an almost quirky vibe, owing to the modernness and sheer difference
of the building's architecture.
