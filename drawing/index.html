Drawing

When I drew this building, I tried to focus on the fact that the pattern imprinted on the outside of the building
with steel beams is not a consistent pattern. At first glance it might seem so, however upon studying the building closely
in order to draw an accurate depiction, I noticed that the gaps between these steels crossbeams get larger as you go higher
up in the building. There could be a myriad of different metaphors one could draw from this. For instance, it could represent
the class system in China (and most of the world), where there are many people clustered together on the bottom of the class ladder
while a few flourish on top. Metaphors aside, I think it is a very interesting design decision, and I think it is aesthetically pleasing.

Upon further research, the diamonds (called diagrids) are not a metaphor, but rather a visual representation of the engineering that
went into building the CCTV tower. "[the diagrids] become dense in areas of greater stress and looser and more open in areas requiring less support"
